Using the GLB Walkthrough in VR

The viewer supports WebXR headsets like the Meta Quest 2 — no app install required.

Getting in

Open this site in the headset's built-in browser (e.g. the Quest Browser) and go to the 3D Model Viewer page. Because WebXR requires a secure connection, this only works over HTTPS — which is exactly how this site is hosted, so no extra setup is needed.

Load a model (or press Load Demo Room to try it immediately), then press Enter VR in the top-right corner and put on your headset.

Quest 2 Controls

Move & look

Left stick moves. Right stick snap-turns your view in fixed increments.

Interact

Trigger opens doors/flips switches when aimed at one, or teleports you to the floor in Teleport mode.

Grab

Grip picks up a grabbable part; squeeze again (or move away and release) to let go. Dropped parts fall and settle naturally.

Mode & reset

A cycles Walk / Fly / Teleport mode. X resets the whole scene — parts, doors, switches, and your position.

Good to know

Keep models under roughly 1M triangles and 100 MB for a comfortable frame rate on Quest 2 — decimate or compress heavier models before loading if things feel sluggish.

The in-scene part editor and the Claude command bar are flat-screen-only features. Make any edits on desktop first, export them, and the same overrides file will drive your VR session.
