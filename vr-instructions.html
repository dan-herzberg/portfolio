Using the GLB Walkthrough in VR

The viewer supports WebXR headsets like the Meta Quest 2 — no app install required.

Getting in

Open this site in the headset's built-in browser (e.g. the Quest Browser) and go to the 3D Model Viewer page. Because WebXR requires a secure connection, this only works over HTTPS — which is exactly how this site is hosted, so no extra setup is needed.

Load a model (or press Load Demo Room to try it immediately), then press Enter VR in the top-right corner and put on your headset.

Loading your own .glb on the headset: the Quest Browser's file picker only shows a flat list of whatever's already sitting in Downloads — it can't browse Internal Storage, an SD card, or cloud drives the way a native app can. Get your model into Downloads first (open a direct download link to it in the Quest Browser, or use the Meta Quest mobile app's "send to headset" feature), and it'll show up in the Load picker.

Quest 2 Controls

Move & look

Left stick moves. Right stick turns smoothly (continuous rotation, not a snap).

Interact

Trigger opens doors/flips switches when aimed at one.

Grab

Grip picks up a grabbable part; squeeze again (or move away and release) to let go. While holding a part, the right stick forward/back pushes it away or pulls it closer. Dropped parts fall and settle naturally.

Fly & teleport

Walking is the default — there's no mode to switch. A jumps; double-tap it to toggle fly (right stick then controls altitude instead). While walking, hold the right stick forward to preview a teleport target and release to go there.

Wrist menu & reset

A menu is locked to your left controller at all times; poke its buttons with your right hand. X resets the whole scene — parts, doors, switches, and your position.

Good to know

Keep models under roughly 1M triangles and 100 MB for a comfortable frame rate on Quest 2 — decimate or compress heavier models before loading if things feel sluggish.

The in-scene part editor and the Claude command bar are flat-screen-only features. Make any edits on desktop first, export them, and the same overrides file will drive your VR session.
